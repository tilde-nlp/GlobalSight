Shifting Web Demographics
Although the Web holds the promise of reaching a worldwide audience instantly, it is predominately an English language medium. Within the last 5 years, more than 200 countries have become connected to the internet. However, global accessibility is not synonymous with global communication.

The demographics of the online community is shifting quickly. Today, more than 50 percent of all Web users originate from outside the US, and by 2005, non-US Web users are expected to account for 70% of the global online community. The majority of this growth will be concentrated in Europe and the Asia-Pacific region, where English is not the primary language.

These trends have tremendous implications for business. To remain competitive in today's wired global economy, companies are faced with the challenge of developing an international Web presence - one that communicates in the languages and cultures of its target audience.

However, the Web itself presents a host of challenges to companies seeking to global their Web sites. Find out more about the challenges of Web globalization.

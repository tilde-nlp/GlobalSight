PeopleSoft, Inc., announced that its newly launched PeopleSoft 8 implements the Unicode Standard in all core components of its architecture. PeopleSoft 8 is a new generation of pure Internet applications, including Internet Customer Relationship Management, Supply Chain Management and Enterprise Management solutions.

PeopleSoft's comprehensive support for Unicode will allow customers to centrally maintain and manage implementations of PeopleSoft 8 in virtually every modern language in a single database. This will enable multinational companies to better coordinate and streamline operations.

    Turkish and Greek T9 Text Input Available
 
Tegic Communications announced the availability of Turkish T9 Text Input, its latest language database. With the addition of Turkish and Greek, T9 Text Input is now available in 16 languages and 18 databases, including all major European and Asian languages, Chinese, Danish, Dutch, English, Finnish, French, German, Italian, Japanese, Korean (Hangul), Norwegian, Portuguese, Spanish and Swedish.

    64-bit Chinese Linux Developed
 
In collaboration with Singapore-based Donovan Systems, China's Institute of Software has unveiled the first 64-bit Chinese Linux operating system. Donovan Systems shared its knowledge and expertise of 64-bit UltraSPARC processing technology and contributed towards the development cost of Chinese Penguin64.

Chinese Penguin64 has double-byte capacity in its kernel. Its Chinese processing capability covers input, data processing, printing and display. Chinese Penguin64 supports a range of industry and international standards, including SPARC V8 and V9, the X11R6 interactive input standard XIM and all Solaris binary code applications. The system comes complete with Internet applications, including Web and e-mail server software, fax, file and print services. It supports all common Internet standards, Web-enabled remote network and system services as well as SSL security.

"Linux has a huge following in China, and the open-source nature of Chinese Penguin64 will prove to be highly popular as end-users can freely adapt it to their own needs," said the deputy director of the Institute of Software, Professor Li Mingshu.

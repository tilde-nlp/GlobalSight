How does GlobalSight Word Count work

Word counting is an inexact science. Every word processor, MS Word, WordPerfect, FrameMaker, counts words differently. For example, some word processors consider numbers to be words, others do not.

 

GlobalSight defines a word as any number of contiguous non-whitespace characters. GlobalSight does not count non-printing characters such as spaces, paragraph marks, line feed characters, tabs, or words that start with a number.

 

Not all words that are available for counting are counted, though. For example, Internal Localizables are not counted because they don't need to be translated.

 

External Localizables, on the other hand, are given a Word Count of 1 regardless of the number of words in the segment because these segments require minimal attention from translators therefore not justifying a full word count. So why count them at all since they require minimal attention from translators? Because we want the Word Count to be representative of the page, since many pages are almost all localizable data. This give translators a better estimate of how long it will take to process the page, even if they will not be translating most of the data.
